Observability 101

Ever wondered what all those "ms" numbers actually mean? Let's break down how the internet works using simple analogies.

D
DNS Resolution

Computers don't understand names like google.com; they only understand numbers called IP addresses. DNS is the system that translates names into numbers.

"Think of DNS like looking up a friend's name in a phonebook to find their number before you can call them."
C
Connect (TCP)

Once we have the number, we need to pick up the phone and establish a line. This is the "Connection" phase where your computer and the server say hello.

"It's like dialing the number and waiting for the other person to say 'Hello?'"
T
TLS Handshake

On the modern web, everything is encrypted for your safety. TLS is the process where both sides agree on a 'secret code' to talk in so no one can overhear.

"Think of it like two spies meeting in a park and exchanging a secret code-book before they start whispering."
B
TTFB (Time to First Byte)

This is often the most important number. It's the time it takes for the server to actually process your request and send the very first piece of information back.

"Imagine ordering food at a restaurant. TTFB is the time between you handing the menu to the waiter and the waiter bringing out your first appetizer."
K
Keep-Alive

Pingtym uses 'World Class' connection reuse. Instead of hanging up the phone and redialing every minute, we keep the line open.

"It's like staying on the line with a friend instead of hanging up and calling back every time you have one more thing to say."
